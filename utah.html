Utah

Utah’s primary native trout are four subspecies of Cutthroat trout (Bonneville, Bear Lake, Colorado River, and Yellowstone), recognized by red jaw slashes. The state also features popular non-native trout, including Rainbow, Brown, Brook, Lake, and Hybrid Tiger trout, widely stocked across its rivers, streams, and high mountain lakes.

Spring

This period brings intense hatches of mayflies and caddis, particularly the Blue Winged Olive (BWO) hatch.

Flies

  • BWO nymphs/duns (#18-22)
  • Mother Shucker
  • Parachute Adams (#20-24)
  • Spanish Bullet Perdigon (#14-18)
  • Black Stoneflies (#20-24)

Species

  • Bonneville Cutthroat Trout
  • Rainbow Trout
  • Brown Trout
  • Cutbow (Cutthroat/Rainbow hybrid)
  • Mountain Whitefish

Location

  • Lower elevation rivers and tailwaters (Provo River, Weber River)
  • Spring-fed creeks and slower runs
  • Areas with consistent flows below dams
  • Banks, seams, and softer water where fish conserve energy during runoff

Summer

Dry fly action peaks with the Green Drake hatch, stoneflies, and terrestrials.

Flies

  • Green Drakes
  • PMX (Parachute Madam X - olive/royal, #10-14)
  • Chernobyl Ant (tan, #10)
  • Morrish Hopper (#10)
  • Elk Hair Caddis (#14-18)
  • Rubber-legged Copper John (#12-18)

Species

  • Bonneville Cutthroat Trout
  • Rainbow Trout
  • Brown Trout (more active early/late)
  • Brook Trout (higher elevations)
  • Tiger Trout (stocked waters)

Location

  • High mountain lakes and alpine streams
  • Faster riffles and oxygen-rich pocket water
  • Deep pools and shaded banks during mid-day heat
  • Early morning and evening near structure (undercut banks, logs, boulders)

fall

A fantastic time for big dry flies and productive streamer fishing as fish become aggressive.

Flies

  • PMX
  • Parachute Hopper
  • Foam Ants/Beetles
  • Wooly Buggers
  • Cicada patterns
  • Mahogany Mayflies

Species

  • Brown Trout (spawning, very aggressive)
  • Rainbow Trout
  • Bonneville Cutthroat Trout
  • Brook Trout (spawning in higher elevations)
  • Mountain Whitefish

Location

  • Rivers and streams with spawning runs (riffles and gravel beds)
  • Undercut banks, log jams, and deeper pools
  • Transition zones between fast and slow water
  • Lower elevation rivers as temperatures begin to drop

Winter

Fishing is best during warmer afternoon hours, focusing on nymphs and midges.

Flies

  • Zebra Midge (black, red, olive, #20-24)
  • San Juan Worms (red, wine, #18-20)
  • Sow Bugs (grey, #18-24)
  • RS2s (grey, black, #20-22)
  • Egg Patterns (peach, orange, #14-18)
  • Wooly Buggers (white, black, #4-8)

Species

  • Rainbow Trout
  • Brown Trout
  • Bonneville Cutthroat Trout
  • Mountain Whitefish

Location

  • Deep, slow-moving pools and tailwaters
  • Areas below dams with stable water temperatures
  • Soft seams and slower currents where fish conserve energy
  • Deeper runs away from heavy current
