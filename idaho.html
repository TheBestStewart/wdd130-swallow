Idaho

Idaho boasts a diverse range of trout species, including native Cutthroat trout, Redband trout, Bull trout, and migratory Steelhead, alongside introduced Rainbow trout, Brook trout, Brown trout, and Lake trout.

Spring

Flies

  • March–April: Blue Winged Olives (BWOs) and midges dominate. Small nymphs (#18–22) and midge patterns are highly effective.
  • Early runoff: San Juan Worms, Squirmy Worms, and egg patterns excel in higher, off-color water.
  • Pre-runoff windows: Skwala stoneflies (#10–14) provide excellent dry fly opportunities, especially on freestone rivers.
  • General producers: Pheasant Tail, Hare’s Ear, and Zebra Midge patterns (#16–22) consistently produce.

Species

  • Rainbow Trout
  • Brown Trout
  • Cutthroat Trout (Westslope & Yellowstone)
  • Cutbow (Cutthroat/Rainbow hybrid)
  • Mountain Whitefish

Location

  • Tailwaters and spring creeks (Henry’s Fork, Silver Creek)
  • Lower elevation rivers before peak runoff
  • Slow seams, eddies, and inside bends
  • Banks and softer water where fish hold during higher flows

Summer

Flies

  • June: Salmonfly and Golden Stoneflynymphs are essential as runoff subsides. Green Drakes start to appear on many waters.
  • July: Peak season for Caddis and Pale Morning Duns (PMDs). Chubby Chernobylsand Stimulators are highly effective during the stonefly hatch.
  • August: Hopper patterns (Fat Albert, Thunder Thighs) are critical, especially near grassy banks. Ants and beetles are effective terrestrials.

Species

  • Rainbow Trout
  • Brown Trout
  • Cutthroat Trout
  • Brook Trout (high elevation streams)
  • Mountain Whitefish

Location

  • High mountain lakes and alpine streams
  • Riffles, runs, and oxygen-rich pocket water
  • Undercut banks, log jams, and shaded structure
  • Early morning and evening near feeding lanes

Fall

Flies

  • September: Terrestrials continue to work early in the month. Blue Winged Olivesappear on cloudy days, and Mahogany Dunsemerge.
  • October: October Caddis are a staple. Baetis (BWO) nymphs and dry flies are essential, especially in slower, deeper pools.
  • November: Focus on midges and BWOhatches, particularly on tailwaters like Silver Creek.

Species

  • Brown Trout (spawning, very aggressive)
  • Rainbow Trout
  • Cutthroat Trout
  • Brook Trout (spawning in small streams)
  • Mountain Whitefish

Location

  • Rivers with spawning activity (riffles and gravel beds)
  • Undercut banks, deep pools, and structure
  • Transition water between fast and slow currents
  • Lower elevation rivers as temperatures cool

Winter

Flies

  • December - February: The best options are small nymphs such as Zebra Midges (red or black), Pheasant Tails, and Copper Johns. Egg patterns are effective in areas with spawning whitefish.

Species

  • Rainbow Trout
  • Brown Trout
  • Cutthroat Trout
  • Mountain Whitefish

Location

  • Tailwaters and spring creeks with stable temperatures
  • Deep, slow pools and runs
  • Soft seams and slower currents
  • Deeper holding water away from fast flow

General Idaho Fly Selection By Type

  • Dry Flies: Parachute Adams, Elk Hair Caddis, Purple Haze, Chubby Chernobyl.
  • Nymphs: Pheasant Tail, Hare’s Ear, Zebra Midge, Pat’s Rubber Legs.
  • Streamers: Woolly Buggers (olive/black), Sculpin patterns.
